Charter Purpose & Strategic Value

Charter Purpose

Define the scope, governance, success criteria, and workstream structure for deploying Knapsack's workspace expansion across PNC's enterprise design system teams. This charter enables Ledger to evolve from a web-centric documentation platform into an intelligent, multi-platform delivery engine that serves native mobile (iOS, Android), web, and future platforms with unified governance and component discovery.

Strategic Value

By expanding Knapsack workspaces across PNC's design system ecosystem, we establish:

  • Multi-Platform Component Registry: Native component showcase rendering (SwiftUI, Kotlin) alongside web components in a single source of truth
  • Token & Design System Parity: Platform-specific token configuration and versioning with cross-platform consistency validation
  • Enterprise Accessibility Standards: Unified accessibility framework (WCAG 2.1 AA) enforced across all platforms and workspaces
  • Coordinated Governance: Mobile-first governance and documentation integration with clear ownership across 9 concurrent workstreams
  • Scalable Discovery: Enterprise-scale component registration and adoption analytics enabling teams to find, reuse, and contribute components at scale

Expected Outcomes

Successful deployment of 9 concurrent Knapsack workspaces will enable:

  • 70%+ adoption of shared components across digital teams
  • 85%+ cross-platform design and token consistency
  • 40% reduction in design-to-development time through component reuse
  • 100% WCAG 2.1 AA compliance across all platforms
  • Unified governance reducing design drift and maintenance overhead

Formalize backlog prioritization, automate reporting through Jira, and implement performance metrics to improve transparency and system health tracking.

Risk Management

Discussion Questions for PNC Leadership

Which risks resonate most with your experience? Are there organizational or infrastructure risks we haven't identified?

How do we establish early warning indicators for resource contention and adoption issues to course-correct quickly?

9 Concurrent Workstream Risks & Mitigation

Risk Impact Likelihood Mitigation Strategy
Cloud authoring instability blocking go-live High High Forward-Deployed Engineering + DevOps Audit engagement. Early POC validation. SLA requirements.
Resource contention across 9 teams High High Strategic Change Management for prioritization + governance pacing. Clear RACI + resource planning. Phased sequencing to avoid peaks.
Inconsistent adoption maturity across workspaces Medium High Tailored enablement under Knapsack Strongly Recommended Services. Workspace-specific training + champions. Peer mentoring.
Token + component drift across platforms High Medium Token Strategy + System Architecture definition. Automated drift detection. Regular alignment ceremonies. Clear governance.
Governance fatigue and decision paralysis Medium Medium Strategic Change Management workshops. Clear governance framework. Decision protocols + escalation paths. Regular stakeholder alignment.
Mobile workspace dependencies blocking rollout Medium Medium Forward-Deployed Engineering for mobile-specific blockers. Early identification + mitigation. Parallel track planning.
Change management overload (9 streams) High Medium Strategic Change Management: coordinated roadmap + stakeholder alignment. Standardized messaging. Champions network.
Analytics inconsistency across workspaces Low Medium Design System Analytics standardization. Consistent instrumentation. Regular data validation + reconciliation.

Overall Risk Profile

High Complexity: 9 concurrent workstreams present elevated resource contention and coordination risks. Mitigations emphasize:

  • Forward-Deployed Engineering for technical blockers (especially cloud authoring + mobile dependencies)
  • Strategic Change Management services for adoption + governance coordination
  • Clear sequencing and RACI to manage resource contention
  • Tailored enablement to address adoption variance across workspaces
