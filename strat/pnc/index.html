Design System North Star Charter: Vision + Implementation Strategy

Strategic Foundation

North Star Vision

Ledger is how PNC designs and builds with confidence — enabling customer-centric, data-driven experiences at scale.

At its core, this is an enterprise design system that turns the complex into the simple — allowing PNC to scale consistency, quality, and accessibility — across all digital experiences.

Design System Team Mission

We enable and empower the creators of PNC digital products by breaking down the barriers of design systems to deliver a simple, smart, and empathetic experience for PNC customers.

Our mission is to make the right way the easy way: to help teams move faster, reduce complexity, and deliver with confidence, every time.

Core Principles

Build once, share everywhere — because unity makes strength.

Value-Driven

Iterative Excellence

Accessible by Design

Value Proposition

We built Ledger so you don't have to — saving time, reducing cost, and improving quality. Each product team benefits from consistent patterns and lower technical risk.

Why Teams Choose Ledger

  • Faster: Reusable components, starter kits, and automated workflows reduce design time.
  • Safer: Governance-as-a-service ensures compliance, accessibility, and design integrity.
  • Smarter: Shared data, patterns, and insights enable better decisions and higher ROI.

At PNC, we don't chase trends — we build things that last. Ledger reflects values that define partnership philosophy: we believe in saying "Yes, and..." because collaboration fuels momentum. We lead with empathy, teach with patience, and guide with evidence.

2025-2026 Strategic Objectives

The Design System North Star Charter represents a critical inflection point for scaling Ledger's value across PNC's digital landscape.

1. Reduce Fragmentation & Technical Debt

Unify Ledger and Ledger Private Bank libraries. Document, align, and retire legacy platforms to reduce maintenance overhead and design drift.

2. Complete Token & Theme Expansion

Extend the token system to native platforms. Establish parity between web and native platforms (iOS/Android) through a unified framework.

3. Deliver Material 3 Upgrade

Complete rollout by December 2025, ensuring stability, performance, and post-launch refinement guided by user feedback and accessibility validation.

4. Enable Scalable Workspaces

Implement SSO adoption, Figma linkage, and configure governance across at PNC design environments for streamlined adoption.

5. Advance the Mobile Foundation

Integrate SwiftUI and Kotlin codebases into the enterprise design system. Define mobile-specific accessibility, testing, and documentation standards.

6. Strengthen the Governance Framework

Formalize backlog RACI analysis, automate reporting through Jira Insight, and implement performance metrics to improve transparency and system health tracking.

Implementation Strategy

The following work streams and supporting infrastructure are designed to achieve the North Star Vision and deliver the 2026-2027 Strategic Objectives.

Phase 1: Current State Assessment + 2026 PI Planning

Establish baseline understanding of documentation fragmentation, tool transitions, and component registration readiness. This phase informs the prioritization and sequencing of delivery activities.

Requirements & Gap Analysis

  • Document all web and mobile technical capabilities
  • Identify component registration dependencies
  • Assess current state vs. future requirements

Stakeholder Alignment

  • Establish governance & cadence (quarterly PI Planning)
  • Define success metrics and adoption KPIs
  • Secure cross-functional commitment

Phase 2: Funded Delivery (December 2025)

Execute on contracted deliverables that directly support the North Star Vision and enable mobile-first thinking across PNC's digital ecosystem.

Native Showcase Rendering

  • SwiftUI and Kotlin showcase applications
  • Web client documentation hub for mobile teams
  • Component registration model and central registry

Phase 3: Roadmap Investment (2026)

Requires separate approval and resource allocation. Quarterly initiatives designed to deepen mobile capability and governance maturity.

Mobile Token Configuration

  • Platform-specific token units (sp, dp, pt)
  • Token versioning and pipeline integration
  • Centralized accessibility guidelines (WCAG 2.1 AA)

Mobile Figma Integration

  • Figma libraries for iOS and Android components
  • Auto-sync between design and native codebases

Token Versioning

  • Versioned token releases with changelog management
  • Pipeline integration for automated distribution

In-App Documentation

  • Embedded documentation within native applications
  • Just-in-time guidance for developers

Automated Accessibility Validation

  • Accessibility testing automation in CI/CD pipeline
  • Performance metrics and governance dashboard

Systems of Systems Architecture

  • Design and validate architecture without disrupting business as usual workflows
  • Establish federated component governance model to scale across PNC divisions
  • Future-proof information architecture to support growth beyond current platform constraints
  • Define clear separation of concerns between core Ledger system and domain-specific implementations
  • Enable seamless integration with legacy systems during transition period
Critical Success Factors: Component registration from PNC repositories (critical path), Knapsack Forward Deployed Engineer support, and Future-Proof Token Strategy implementation. Success is measured not by deliverables shipped, but by how loudly we promote Ledger, by how quietly it works, and how much time it saves, how much rework it prevents, and how confidently teams choose it because it simply makes sense.
Constraints & Guardrails

Constraints

  • Component configs: Restricted inputs, switches, parameters to enable customization but not alter the UI.
  • Design option packages: Clear examples of components and variants
  • Restricted component inputs and customization parameters.
  • Defined variant limits for maintainability.
  • Consistent naming conventions across organizations.
  • Periodic CX and UX alignment sessions.
  • Positive reinforcement from leadership to sustain culture.

Guardrails

  • Regular review of app-level CX to ensure system is serving customer needs
  • Regular strategic vision sharing with UX leaders
  • Libertarian paternalism as default mode — guide without restricting
  • Positive words on the system from levels up in the organization
  • Consistent naming convention for design and development
  • Consistent naming across Private Bank and Retail DS/CL divisions

Top Risks & Mitigations

  • Delayed component builds → Proactive MVP delivery with SLAs to maintain momentum and team confidence
  • Ad-hoc prioritization → Enforce governance via triage & RAID to align requests with strategic objectives
  • Token adoption resistance → Pilot in key components and share measurable impact to build adoption culture
  • Feedback overload → Structured intake and batch triage process to manage demand and maintain team velocity
  • Negative perception of governance → Shift messaging from control to collaboration to emphasize partnership and shared success
