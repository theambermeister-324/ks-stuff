Statement of Work No. 1

Allegion Design System Initiative

Knapsack Professional Services – CX Partnership & Technical Implementation

Background & Terms

Project Context

Knapsack will support Allegion's 2026 enterprise design system initiative through two coordinated workstreams. This engagement aligns to Allegion's critical WIN themes:

  1. Efficiency & Speed
  2. Scalability & Consistency Across 10+ Brands
  3. AI Readiness & Innovation

This SOW provides: (A) CX Partnership for strategy, governance, and AI advisory, and (B) Implementation Workstream for technical enablement and deployment support.

AI Advisory Role

Knapsack will act as Allegion's AI advisor, offering guidance, frameworks, and workflow recommendations. Knapsack will not implement or deploy AI systems in Allegion's environment. Allegion retains all responsibility for AI system implementation.

Capitalized terms not otherwise defined herein have the meanings set forth in the Customer Agreement. Description & Assumptions

Scope of Services

Knapsack will deliver two coordinated workstreams to support Allegion's unified design system across 10+ brands with multi-stack complexity.

Workstream A — CX Partnership (Strategy, Governance & AI Advisory)

Knapsack's CX Partner will guide governance, strategic alignment, design system operating model definition, and development of Allegion's AI-Enabled Operating Model.

Services Included:

  • Design System Charter development
  • Governance Framework definition
  • Multi-Brand Operating Model design
  • Contribution Processes & Templates
  • Change Management & Communication Plan
  • UX & Content Training
  • KPI & Measurement Framework
  • AI-Enabled Operating Model (Advisory)

AI-Enabled Operating Model (Advisory):

Knapsack will advise on how Allegion can:

  • Prepare components, tokens, design patterns, and workflows for AI augmentation
  • Govern AI-assisted workflows
  • Evolve manual processes toward AI-supported practices over time
  • Structure future AI capability growth

Note: Allegion retains all responsibility for AI system implementation.

Workstream B — Implementation Workstream (Technical Enablement)

Due to multi-brand, multi-stack complexity, a 10% FTE Forward-Deployed Engineer (FDE) is required for 12 months to provide technical enablement activities.

Services Included:

  • Environment preparation and validation
  • Local install support
  • Component rendering support
  • Rendering SME guidance
  • Deployment support
  • DevOps/CI/CD SME support
  • Cloud authoring setup & testing
  • SSO setup coordination
  • Engineering training
  • UX & Content training
  • Transition support

Key Assumptions

Customer Responsibilities:

  • Allegion owns implementation of design, engineering, and AI systems
  • Provide stakeholder access, documentation, and timely approvals
  • Supply SME availability for technical coordination
  • Manage all code repositories, infrastructure, and system integrations
  • Own all updates to design assets, code components, and token implementations

Knapsack Responsibilities:

  • Provide strategic guidance, CX partnership, and AI advisory only
  • Provide FDE technical enablement support for implementation complexity
  • Not perform production deployment or AI system implementation

Project Dependencies:

  • Customer approvals and SME availability impact project timelines
  • FDE support is required due to implementation complexity

Activities

Key Activities:

  • AI Operating Model definition (advisory)

  • Brand-specific training sessions

Phase 3 — Deployment Enablement & Workflow Readiness

Months 6–8

Objective: Deployment workflows, CI/CD validation, QA support, engineering enablement, and refinement of AI-ready processes (advisory).

Key Activities:

  • Deployment workflow configuration
  • CI/CD pipeline validation and support
  • DevOps SME guidance
  • QA and testing support
  • Engineering enablement sessions
  • AI readiness roadmap and use case modeling

  • Audit framework for future AI-powered reviews

Phase 4 — Enterprise Adoption & Model Reinforcement

Months 9–11

Objective: Persona-specific training, adoption support, governance reinforcement, communication, and change management activities.

Key Activities:

  • Adoption support and office hours
  • Governance charter finalization
  • Maintenance and audit process documentation
  • Change management campaigns
  • Stakeholder communication plans
  • Success story documentation

Phase 5 — Optimization & Transition / Future-State Planning

Month 12

Objective: Transition documentation, handoff to Allegion teams, optimization planning, and future AI/design system roadmap (advisory).

Key Activities:

  • Transition documentation and knowledge transfer

  • Optimization recommendations
  • KPI dashboard and adoption measurement strategy

  • Future-state AI and design system roadmap (advisory)

Appendix: Supporting Information

A. WIN Theme Details

WIN Theme 1: Efficiency & Speed

Focus on accelerating product development, eliminating redundant work, and enabling teams to deliver faster with reusable components and clear guidelines.

WIN Theme 2: Scalability & Consistency Across 10+ Brands

Establish unified design standards that accommodate multiple brand identities while maintaining consistency and enabling scale without proportional headcount increases.

WIN Theme 3: AI Readiness & Innovation

Future-proof the design system for AI-augmented workflows, including automated component generation, intelligent audits, and design operations optimization.
