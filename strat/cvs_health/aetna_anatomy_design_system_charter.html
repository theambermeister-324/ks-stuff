Executive Summary

1. Charter Purpose

This document outlines a hypothetical system charter for the Aetna Anatomy Design System—the design system that serves as the foundation for consistent user experiences across Aetna Healthcare's 9 digital properties. This charter hypothesis defines the design system's purpose, scope, governance model, and how it enables better management, consistency, and operational efficiency across Aetna's diverse portfolio.

Design System Vision

Aetna Anatomy Design System provides a unified design language, component library, and governance framework that enables consistent, accessible, and efficient digital experiences across all Aetna properties—from core consumer-facing sites (Aetna.com, Aetna.com/Medicare) to specialty portals (Dental, International, Student Health, Better Health of Maryland).

2. Strategic Context

Why This Design System Exists

Aetna Healthcare operates 9 distinct digital properties serving diverse audiences (consumers, Medicare members, Medicaid members, international members, students, dental plan members). Without a unified design system:

  • Inconsistency: Each property develops its own patterns, creating brand fragmentation
  • Duplication: Teams rebuild similar components across properties, wasting engineering capacity
  • Accessibility Risk: Inconsistent implementation increases compliance risk and defect rates
  • Operational Cost: Manual processes, fragmented governance, and lack of visibility increase operational overhead
  • Velocity Impact: Teams spend time on rework instead of innovation

Market Forces

Headwinds

  • $1.2B cost-cutting initiative
  • Regulatory compliance scrutiny
  • Accessibility lawsuit escalation
  • Organizational restructuring

Tailwinds

  • Cost reduction mandate creates urgency for efficiency
  • Proven design system foundation exists
  • Clear property ownership (9 properties)
  • Expansion model potential

Governance & Ownership

4. Design System Governance Model

Ownership Structure

The Aetna Design System Team owns and manages the Aetna Anatomy Design System. This team is responsible for:

  • Design system strategy and roadmap
  • Component design and development
  • Governance and quality standards
  • Documentation and developer enablement
  • Usage tracking and compliance

Governance Principles

Design System Team Authority

  • Final approval on component additions/modifications
  • Governance enforcement across properties
  • Quality standards definition
  • Accessibility compliance oversight

Property Team Responsibilities

  • Adhere to design system standards
  • Report component gaps and needs
  • Participate in design system reviews
  • Provide usage feedback

Contribution Model

Property teams can propose new components or modifications, but all changes must go through the Design System Team's governance process:

  1. Proposal: Property team submits component request
  2. Review: Design System Team evaluates need, scope, and impact
  3. Design: Design System Team designs component (or approves property design)
  4. Development: Design System Team develops component (or approves property implementation)
  5. Documentation: Component documented in design system
  6. Distribution: Component made available to all properties

10. Accessibility as a Use Case

Why Accessibility Governance Matters

Accessibility compliance is a critical requirement for healthcare digital properties. The design system can serve as the foundation for "accessible by design" practices:

  • Component-Level Compliance: Design system components built to WCAG standards
  • Usage Validation: Track how components are used to ensure accessibility
  • Defect Prevention: Identify accessibility issues before they reach production
  • Compliance Reporting: Demonstrate compliance across properties
Use Case Hypothesis

Accessibility governance could serve as a focused use case to demonstrate the value of better design system management. By tracking component usage and accessibility compliance across properties, the design system team can identify patterns, prevent defects, and demonstrate ROI before broader platform adoption.
