Aetna-First Pilot Approach

This charter focuses specifically on Aetna Healthcare's accessibility challenges as the pilot engagement. By proving value with Aetna's 9+ digital properties first, we establish a model that can expand to Caremark, CVS Pharmacy, and other CVS Health entities. This Aetna-first approach allows us to demonstrate measurable accessibility governance and risk reduction while respecting organizational boundaries and building trust through proof.

Strategic Context

1. Charter Purpose

Define the scope, governance, success criteria, and workstream structure for deploying Knapsack's platform to establish accessibility governance across Aetna Healthcare's digital properties. This Aetna-focused charter establishes the foundation for "Accessible by Design" practices that reduce legal risk, operational costs, and executive escalations—with a clear pathway to expand proven value to other CVS Health entities.

2. Business Objectives

Primary Objectives (Aetna-Focused)

  1. Reduce Aetna Accessibility Defects: Establish governance framework to prevent accessibility violations before they reach production, reducing YTD defect patterns across Aetna's 9+ digital properties (Aetna.com, Medicare, International, Better Health, Dental, Student Health, etc.)
  2. Quantify Aetna Cost Reduction: Demonstrate measurable reduction in accessibility team remediation costs, legal risk, and executive escalation incidents specific to Aetna properties
  3. Enable Aetna Pattern Recognition: Identify common accessibility defects across Aetna's multiple delivery tech stacks (React/Angular/native) and tie them to shared components
  4. Build Aetna Governance Foundation: Create reusable governance templates and validation workflows proven with Aetna that can scale to other CVS Health entities
  5. Prove Value Within 90 Days: Deliver auditable metrics showing Aetna-specific risk reduction and cost savings to inform procurement decision
  6. Establish Expansion Model: Create a replicable approach that demonstrates value for Caremark, CVS Pharmacy, and other entities without requiring centralized control

Aetna-Specific Pain Points

The Aetna Challenge

Fragmented Properties, Common Defects: Aetna operates 9+ distinct digital properties (non-marketing sites under Nathan Frank) with shared accessibility defect patterns that cannot be easily identified or prevented. Multiple tech stacks (React/Angular/native) create complexity, but common components suggest governance can prevent defects before they propagate.

Key Insight: If accessibility defects are showing up across properties, teams are either not using design system components or using them incorrectly. Governance can address both root causes.

2a. Market Forces & Strategic Context Analysis

Current Market Dynamics Impacting Aetna

Regulatory Compliance Pressure

  • Transparency in Coverage (TiC) Non-Compliance: Aetna, along with Cigna and UnitedHealthcare, has been called out for failing to fully adhere to TiC rule requiring public disclosure of negotiated rates (Dec 2025 study)
  • Accessibility Legal Risk: Healthcare accessibility lawsuits are increasing; ADA/WCAG compliance failures can result in significant settlements and reputational damage
  • Regulatory Costs: Major insurers face compliance costs exceeding $1B annually; non-compliance penalties add to financial pressure

Financial Performance Challenges

  • ACA Market Losses: Aetna reported $924M adjusted operating income loss from ACA exchange products in 2024; exiting ACA markets by 2026
  • Medical Loss Ratio Volatility: MLR fluctuated from 85.7% (Q3 2023) to 95.2% (Q3 2024), indicating cost management challenges
  • $1.2B Cost-Cutting Initiative: Ongoing operational efficiency drive requires reducing waste and improving delivery speed

Competitive Market Pressures

  • Medicare Advantage Enrollment Decline: Lost ~300K MA members (-5.8%) in 2024 AEP vs. +476K gain previous year; competitors like UnitedHealthcare gained 380K
  • Market Saturation: Over 900 insurers compete nationally; price wars and reduced margins require operational efficiency
  • Technology-Driven Competitors: Companies like Oscar Health leveraging tech-driven strategies to capture market share

Organizational Uncertainty

  • Potential CVS Breakup: CVS reportedly weighing separation of Aetna insurance unit from retail pharmacy division (Oct 2024); creates strategic uncertainty
  • Post-Acquisition Integration: Seven years post-acquisition, still navigating integration challenges between CVS, Aetna, and Caremark
  • Reorganization Impact: Ongoing organizational changes may shift ownership and budget alignment during pilot
