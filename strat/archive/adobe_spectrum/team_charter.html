Vision

To make every Adobe experience feel at home, for everyone, on any surface - through a living, intelligent design system creators can trust.

Mission

We build and steward the Spectrum Application as a product that empowers designers, engineers, contributors, and partners to create cohesive, inclusive, joyful Adobe experiences at scale - powered by Knapsack's platform.

Strategic Context

1. Purpose

The Spectrum Application is Adobe's unified design system delivery platform - the shared visual, interaction, and behavioral foundation for over 100 products across Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, Experience Cloud, Adobe Express, and emerging platforms. The purpose of the Spectrum Application Team is to design, build, evolve, and steward Spectrum as a living, multi-platform product that ensures Adobe experiences are intuitive, inclusive, joyful, and consistent for creators around the world.

We Exist To

  • Provide a single, authoritative source of truth for Adobe's design language
  • Enable teams to design and ship cohesive experiences efficiently, with fewer inconsistencies
  • Ensure Adobe meets and exceeds expectations around accessibility, inclusivity, and quality
  • Support and empower a global ecosystem of practitioners and partners through clear guidance, tooling, and collaboration
  • Adapt and scale the system to meet the needs of new platforms, technologies, and a growing creative community
Critical Understanding

Spectrum is not a reference website. It is a mission-critical product that powers Adobe's entire experience ecosystem.

Scope & Stakeholders

4. Scope

4.1 System Definition & Evolution

  • Visual language: color, typography, iconography, illustration, shape system
  • Interaction patterns, motion, behavior
  • Accessibility standards, inclusive design principles, Product Equity alignment
  • Component systems across platforms (web, iOS, Android, desktop, emerging surfaces)
  • Tokens, semantic foundations, design data models

4.2 Spectrum Application

  • The sole, canonical home for the design system
  • Documentation, guidance, specs, and examples
  • Code implementations and their lifecycle
  • Infrastructure for contribution, versioning, and governance
  • Knapsack platform integration
  • MCP Beta Program participation

4.3 Support & Collaboration

  • Onboarding, education, and enablement for internal teams and partners
  • Key-screen explorations, workflow guidance, prototypes, best practices
  • Feedback loops and structured engagement channels
  • Partner audit support and compliance validation

4.4 Adoption & Quality

  • Establishing metrics for system health, adoption, accessibility, quality
  • Monitoring drift and inconsistencies across Adobe products
  • Supporting migrations to Spectrum 2 and future versions
  • Drift detection and remediation workflows

Roles & Responsibilities

6. Core Team Roles

6.1 Design System Designers Adobe

  • Define and evolve foundations (color, typography, iconography, motion)
  • Design and maintain multi-platform components and patterns
  • Create documentation, examples, diagrams, key-screen explorations
  • Advise product teams, provide feedback, guide system-appropriate solutions
  • Serve as librarians, coaches, ambassadors for Spectrum adoption

6.2 Design Engineers / System Engineers Adobe

  • Build and maintain Spectrum component implementations
  • Ensure parity across frameworks (Web Components, React Spectrum, native)
  • Establish engineering best practices for performance, accessibility
  • Partner on prototyping and technical feasibility exploration
  • Maintain code pipelines, versioning, automated documentation sync

6.3 Content Strategy & Documentation Adobe

  • Own clarity, structure, accessibility of documentation
  • Define editorial standards for naming, guidance tone, instructional content
  • Translate system principles into user-friendly, actionable documentation

6.4 Program & Operations Adobe

  • Drive alignment across clouds, surfaces, partner teams
  • Manage intake, triage, prioritization, governance workflows
  • Maintain roadmap, milestones, release communications
  • Facilitate feedback loops, office hours, support channels

6.5 Accessibility & Product Equity Adobe

  • Ensure all components meet WCAG and Adobe accessibility standards
  • Evaluate systemic impact on users with diverse abilities, languages, cultures
  • Partner with design and engineering to validate inclusivity decisions

6.6 Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) Knapsack

  • Primary technical resource for Knapsack platform
  • Platform configuration, integration, optimization
  • Training and enablement delivery
  • Architecture validation and best practices
  • MCP Beta configuration and workflow development

6.7 Customer Experience Partner Knapsack

  • Change management and adoption strategy
  • Stakeholder alignment and communications
  • Success metrics tracking and reporting
  • Strategic partnership coordination

7. Partner & Product Team Responsibilities

Product Teams

  • Implement Spectrum guidance within their surface/product
  • Provide real-world use cases, feedback, examples
  • Participate in early testing, migration planning, component evaluations
  • Own product-specific adjustments that align to Spectrum intent

Implementation Partners

  • Adopt Spectrum components and patterns for integrations
  • Follow guidelines and accessibility requirements
  • Provide implementation feedback and participate in audits
  • Contribute back improvements and patterns where applicable

All requests and decisions must align to Spectrum's core principles: Accessibility and inclusivity, Platform-native coherence, Scalability and sustainability, User-centricity, Contribution transparency, Maintainability over time.
