PCRI: Product System Readiness Index

The validated assessment framework that measures readiness across 5 pillars and routes to archetypes and investment paths.

PCRI Assessment Framework

A composite assessment that measures readiness to evolve from traditional design systems to governed product systems with AI-enabled delivery capabilities.

Why PCRI Matters for Partners

PCRI is not a generic maturity model. It uniquely combines product system foundation, AI readiness, organizational governance, delivery workflow, and culture into a single, actionable score that:

  • • Predicts engagement success
  • • Routes to the right investment path (J1-J4)
  • • Determines which partner roles are needed
  • • Tracks progress over time with longitudinal measurement

The Five PCRI Pillars

20%

20%

20%

Governance

Decision rights, executive sponsorship, budget authority

15%

25%

Contribution models, psychological safety, information flow

Gated Progression Model

PCRI implements progression gates that prevent misclassification:

Gate 1: Foundation → AI Readiness

If Foundation < 40%, AI Score is discounted by 50%. Without a system of record, AI tools generate output that cannot be governed.

Gate 2: Governance → Delivery

If Governance < 30%, Delivery Score is discounted by 30%. Workflow improvements require governance authority to enforce.

Four Archetypes

PCRI score determines archetype, which routes to the appropriate investment path.

PCRI 75-100

AI workflow expansion, innovation leadership

Archetype Selection Logic

Archetype assignment uses both PCRI score and pillar minimums to prevent misclassification. For example, Transformer requires PCRI ≥ 75 and Foundation ≥ 70% and Governance ≥ 60% and Culture ≥ 60%.

Why Deployment Matters for Partners

D3/D4 accounts require Phase 0 (Deployment & Security) before Foundation can begin. Deployment Specialist partners own this phase. Most regulated accounts are D3—always confirm deployment tier in Diagnostic.

Partnership Phases

Phase-gated delivery with clear exit criteria. Never sell all phases upfront—earn the right to the next phase.

Assessment-Driven Phase Planning: PCRI assessment determines which phases are needed and in what order. This ensures partners are engaged at the right time with the right skills for each phase.

Critical Delivery Principles

  • • Phase-gated delivery: Never sell all phases upfront. Earn the right to the next phase.
  • • Value-based pricing: Never quote hourly rates. Price by outcome, not hours.
  • • Clear exit criteria: Each phase must meet defined success criteria before proceeding.
  • • Trust repair focus: Stabilize first, govern second, expand third.
