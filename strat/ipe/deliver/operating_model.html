Common Objections & Responses

Use these responses to handle common objections during Pre-Qualification. Adapt to the specific context.

Objection

"We already have a design system—why do we need Knapsack?"

Response

Knapsack doesn't replace your design system—it makes it actionable. We help your existing components become the source of truth that AI tools, developers, and designers all reference. Think of us as the intelligence layer on top of what you've built.

Objection

"We're not using AI coding tools yet."

Response

That's actually a great position to be in—you can set up governance before chaos. 73% of enterprises will mandate AI coding tools by 2027. Getting your design system AI-ready now means you'll be prepared when adoption accelerates.

Objection

"We need to focus on other priorities right now."

Response

Understood. What's driving those priorities? If it's developer velocity, design consistency, or scaling AI tools safely, those are exactly the problems we solve. We'd love to understand your timeline and stay connected for when this becomes a priority.

Objection

"This seems expensive for what it does."

Response

Let me share what customers typically see: 40% reduction in design-to-code time, 60% fewer UI inconsistencies in production, and 90% reduction in AI-generated code rework. One prevented production incident from ungoverned AI code often covers the entire annual investment.

Deal Desk

Deal Desk manages active deals through Contracting and Procurement stages. This includes health monitoring, intervention playbooks, and procurement acceleration.

Primary Accountability: Deal Desk owns procurement acceleration and time-to-signature. Every day a deal sits in Contracting without forward progress increases the probability of loss.

J3 Approval Gate: J3 Activation deals ($740K+) require tri-party approval before entering Contracting: GTM Lead (commercial terms), Director of Delivery (resource plan), and Product Lead (scope fit). All three must sign off.

Deal Desk Scope: Deal Desk activates at stage D4 (Contracting/Procurement) and manages deals through Win/Loss. For earlier pipeline stages, see Pipeline Stages.
