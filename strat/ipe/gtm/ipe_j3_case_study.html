The Customer

A Top-20 US Financial Services Company

This Fortune 500 financial services company operates consumer banking, wealth management, and insurance platforms serving 12 million customers. Their digital product organization consists of 8 cross-functional product teams (300+ developers, 45 designers) shipping across web and native mobile. They had invested 3 years in building an internal design system ("Meridian") but struggled to prove its value to executive leadership — and AI adoption was making the problem worse.

Note: This is a composite scenario derived from Knapsack's PCRI benchmark dataset of 158 enterprise assessments and pipeline account profiles in financial services. It represents a typical J3 Activation engagement pattern. Company details are illustrative.

The Challenge

Three Tools, Zero Governance, Growing Risk

When the VP of Engineering mandated AI coding tools (Cursor, Copilot) across all product teams, the design system team saw adoption — and drift — accelerate simultaneously.

Context Fragmentation

Meridian lived in Knapsack. Component examples lived in Storybook. Usage guidelines lived in 200+ Markdown files across 3 repos. AI tools couldn't see any of it.

Invisible Compliance Risk

AI-generated UI components were bypassing WCAG 2.1 AA requirements and internal brand guidelines. Accessibility violations caught in QA doubled in 6 months.

Unprovable Design System ROI

The design system team couldn't measure adoption, prove time savings, or show executive leadership that Meridian was delivering value. Budget renewal was at risk.

"Our developers loved Cursor and Copilot. But every sprint, we spent more time fixing AI-generated components than we saved creating them. The design system team was drowning in rework tickets they couldn't even quantify."

— VP of Design Systems (composite)

The Engagement

J3 Activation: 3 Phases, 40 Weeks

The J3 Activation path delivered transformation in three milestone-gated phases. Each phase earned the right to the next by proving measurable outcomes.

Phase 1

16 weeks · $400K

Foundation

Unified context layer across Knapsack, Storybook, and 200+ Markdown files. Deployed Knapsack on Private Cloud (D3) within the customer's VPC. Established governance policies, access controls, and audit trail infrastructure. Connected MCP to Cursor and Copilot instances across 3 pilot teams.

12 weeks · $275K

Activation

Rolled MCP to all 8 product teams. Activated conflict surfacing between Knapsack and Storybook sources. Implemented WCAG compliance rules in governance layer. Seller Takeaways

Why This Deal Pattern Works

PCRI as Entry Point

The 15-minute PCRI assessment created urgency: the customer was below their Financial Services peers. The gap between their score (63) and industry top quartile (88) gave the champion internal ammunition to justify budget.

Phase-Gated De-Risk

Milestone-gated phases reduced procurement friction. The customer approved $400K for Phase 1 Foundation, then expanded to Phases 2–3 after measurable results. Each phase earned the right to the next.

Champion Enablement

The Design System Lead couldn't prove ROI before IPE. The executive dashboard became their most powerful tool — transforming them from a cost center advocate to a strategic value driver with board-level visibility.

Built-In Expansion

J3 Activation naturally leads to J4 Intelligence. The customer's PCRI movement from 63 to 82 unlocked expansion conversations. Year 2 projected ARR: $600K+ (platform + Tier 3 consumption + second brand).
