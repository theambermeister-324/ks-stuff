Assessment is Our Accelerator

Every engagement starts with a Maturity and Readiness Assessment that:

  • • Meets customers where they are—evaluating their current state across Product System Foundation, AI Readiness, and Organizational Readiness
  • • Defines the right investment path—determining which engagement type matches their maturity and goals
  • • Routes work to the right partner—identifying exactly what skills are needed and when, ensuring partners are matched to opportunities that fit their expertise

This assessment-driven approach ensures partners get clear scope, right-fit opportunities, and engaged customers—before delivery begins.

Based on assessment outcomes, customers are routed to one of these investment paths. Partners deliver services aligned to the specific needs identified in the assessment. For detailed phase descriptions, see the Delivery Model tab.

Full Transformation Most Common

Assessment routes here when: Customer has moderate design system maturity (30-70% adoption) and organizational readiness, but needs full activation and AI enablement.

What it is: Complete implementation from setup through AI enablement. Multi-phase engagement over several months.

Includes: Platform setup → Foundation → AI integration → Full deployment

Timeline: Phase 0 (8-16 weeks, D3/D4 only) → Phase 1 (16-20 weeks) → Phase 2 (12-16 weeks) → Phase 3 (8-12 weeks) = 36-48 weeks (D1/D2) or 44-64 weeks (D3/D4)

Partner routing: Requires FDE, CX Partner, and PM—typically delivered by Agency partners or coordinated teams.

AI Enablement Expansion

Assessment routes here when: Customer already has mature design system and governance (>70% adoption), ready to add AI capabilities.

What it is: For companies already using Knapsack (or other design systems) who want to add AI capabilities.

Includes: Connect AI tools → Measure improvements → Expand usage

Timeline: Phase 3 (8-12 weeks) = 8-12 weeks

Partner routing: Requires MCP integration expertise—typically Independent Contractors with MCP specialization or FDEs with AI workflow experience. See Role Profiles for requirements.

Rapid Activation Fast Track

Assessment routes here when: Mature organization with high readiness scores and urgent business need—can move quickly.

What it is: Quick implementation for mature organizations with urgent needs.

Includes: Assessment → Rapid setup → Fast deployment

Timeline: Compressed Phase 1 + Phase 2 = 20-28 weeks (accelerated delivery)

Partner routing: Requires senior technical skills and rapid execution—typically experienced FDEs or specialized Independent Contractors. See Role Profiles for requirements.

Foundation Build Starting Point

Assessment routes here when: Organization lacks a system of record or has low design system maturity—needs foundational setup first.

What it is: Initial setup for organizations building their first design system foundation.

Includes: Assessment → Platform setup → Basic governance

Timeline: Phase 0 (8-16 weeks, D3/D4 only) → Phase 1 (16-20 weeks) = 16-20 weeks (D1/D2) or 24-36 weeks (D3/D4)

Partner routing: Requires deployment and setup expertise—typically Deployment Specialists for Phase 0, FDEs for implementation, CX Partners for governance. See Role Profiles for requirements.

Delivery Model

Assessment-Driven Phase Planning: As covered in Overview, the Maturity and Readiness Assessment determines which phases are needed and in what order. This ensures partners are engaged at the right time with the right skills for each phase.

Partnership Phases

Engagements are phase-gated with clear exit criteria. Assessment determines which phases are needed. Partners deliver services within these phases:

Phase 0: Deployment & Security D3/D4 Only

Duration: 8-16 weeks | Engagement: Substantial enterprise deployment

Workstreams: Environment provisioning, Security architecture, SSO/SCIM implementation, Vendor security questionnaire, Penetration testing coordination

Exit Criteria: Environment provisioned & accessible, Security review approved, Network connectivity validated, Compliance attestation complete, InfoSec sign-off obtained

Partner Opportunity:

Phase 0 is typically delivered by Deployment Specialist partners who handle enterprise infrastructure, freeing Knapsack's core team to focus on product capabilities. See Partner Types tab for Deployment Specialist details.

Phase 1: Foundation Product System Foundation

Duration: 16-20 weeks | Engagement: Foundation build

Workstreams: Workspace deployment, Entitlement & access setup, Design-to-code connection, Governance framework draft

Exit Criteria: Teams can publish without intervention, Access no longer blocking work, First component shipped to production

Phase 2: Activation Control Plane Activation

Duration: 12-16 weeks | Engagement: Control Plane activation

Workstreams: Token strategy implementation, Governance model activation, Cross-system rules, Signal instrumentation

Exit Criteria: Token strategy approved & operating, Governance model active, Decisions flowing through system

Phase 3: Intelligence AI Enablement Partnership

Duration: 8-12 weeks | Engagement: AI enablement partnership

Workstreams: MCP integration, AI workflow design, Testing architecture, Change validation framework

Exit Criteria: One AI-assisted change ships end-to-end, Teams trust the governed output, Exec ROI story documented

Critical Delivery Principles

  • • Phase-gated delivery: Never sell all phases upfront. Earn the right to the next phase.
  • • Value-based pricing: Never quote hourly rates. Price by outcome, not hours.
  • • Clear exit criteria: Each phase must meet defined success criteria before proceeding.
  • • Trust repair focus: For J3 accounts, stabilize first, govern second, expand third.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the application process take?

The complete process from application to onboarding start typically takes 1-2 weeks. Application review is 3-5 business days, qualification call is scheduled within 1 week, and decision notification is within 2 weeks of application.

What happens if I'm not accepted?

If your application isn't accepted, we'll provide feedback on why and what you could improve. You can reapply after addressing the feedback, typically after 6 months. We also maintain a waitlist for future opportunities.

How are opportunities matched to partners?

Our assessment-driven approach evaluates each customer opportunity and matches it to partners based on: role requirements, expertise level, availability, geographic location, and past performance. Your Partner Success Manager handles matching.

Can I work with multiple customers simultaneously?

Yes, depending on your capacity and the engagement scope. Many partners work on 2-3 engagements concurrently. Your PSM will help manage your pipeline and ensure you're not overcommitted.

What if I need help during an engagement?

Your Partner Success Manager is your first point of contact for any issues. We also provide technical support, access to Knapsack engineering team for complex issues, and a partner community for peer support.

How do I progress to higher compensation tiers?

Tier progression is based on: successful delivery track record, certifications completed, customer satisfaction scores, and engagement volume. Your PSM will review your progress quarterly and discuss tier advancement opportunities.

What training is required vs. optional?

Required: Week 1-4 onboarding training, role-specific certification. Optional: Advanced certifications, specialized tracks (MCP integration, governance frameworks), and ongoing skill development courses.

How do I register a deal?

Log into your partner portal, navigate to Deal Registration, and enter the opportunity details. Your PSM will review and approve within 2 business days. Once approved, you receive 90-day deal protection.
