2. Steering Committee Objective

The Adobe Spectrum + Knapsack Steering Committee provides executive oversight for the Knapsack MVP program, with initial focus on MVP1 content migration and CMS cutover.

This forum is responsible for:

  • Validating MVP1 scope and success criteria
  • Reviewing progress against the migration plan and timeline
  • Identifying and removing blockers and cross-team dependencies
  • Preventing scope creep
  • Serving as formal phase-gate review to approve exits between MVP stages
Escalation Rule

Any RAID item marked High Impact or Phase-Blocking must be reviewed in Steering Committee before delivery proceeds.

5. Success Criteria

Primary Success Metric: Legacy CMS can be safely shut off.

Definition of Done (MVP1 Complete)

Platform accessible via Adobe SSO. Web Components catalog live with 68+ components. All ~90 content items migrated with rendering parity. Pilot users actively using platform weekly (count per DEC-04). NPS survey completed with score of 30 or higher. No unresolved P1 bugs. Legacy CMS redirect plan approved.

6. Phase-Gate Calendar

Each Steering Committee meeting maps to one of three governance states: Checkpoint (progress review, risks surfaced early), Phase Gate (formal approval required to proceed), or Scope Gate (MVP boundaries validated or reset).

DateGate TypeRAID FocusApproval Outcome
Jan 30 Governance Assumptions, early risks MVP1 scope locked, success criteria confirmed, governance ratified
Feb 27 Checkpoint Risks, dependencies Migration approach validated, no critical blockers red, cutover plan viable
Mar 27 Hard Phase Gate Issues, dependencies, cutover risk Approve cutover, begin onboarding, decommission legacy CMS
Apr 24 Scope Gate New risks, scope creep MVP1 formally exited, MVP2 scope approved, new RAID logged separately
Critical Decision Point - March 27

The March 27 Hard Phase Gate is the approval point for CMS cutover. Steering Committee must validate: migration complete, pilot feedback positive (NPS 30+), no P1 bugs, and rollback plan documented. Approval authorizes legacy CMS decommissioning.

9. Steering Committee Decision Rights

Accountable For

  • Approving or rejecting phase exits
  • Re-scoping MVPs when risks exceed tolerance
  • Authorizing timeline changes tied to RAID impacts
  • Removing cross-team blockers
  • Validating success criteria achievement

NOT Responsible For

  • Day-to-day delivery decisions
  • Content prioritization within approved scope
  • Technical implementation choices
  • Individual workstream management
  • Vendor relationship management
