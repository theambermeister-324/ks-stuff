Living at The Holler

The Spirit of This Place

We've hosted families celebrating milestones, friends reconnecting after years apart, and strangers who left as lifelong friends. The Holler works best when guests treat it like a beloved family cabin rather than a hotel. That means enjoying it fully—and leaving it ready for the next crew to do the same.

A Few Requests

  • Be kind to the septic system. Mountain living means a sensitive septic. Please only flush toilet paper—no food, tampons, paper towels, or (we've learned to specify) goldfish.
  • Keep smoke outside. Feel free to enjoy the porches and fire pit, but no smoking indoors.
  • The pool table is sacred. No drinks on or near it, please. We've learned this lesson the hard way.
  • Fires are welcome, but be responsible. Keep fires contained, never leave them unattended, and make sure they're fully extinguished before you turn in.
  • Furniture has a home. If you rearrange things for your gathering, we just ask that you put everything back before you leave.
  • Respect quiet hours (10 PM – 7 AM). We have neighbors who appreciate the mountain peace as much as you do.
  • Park on the driveway, not the lawn. The grass thanks you in advance.
  • Pets are welcome—and so is cleaning up after them. We love furry guests. Just keep an eye on any messes, inside or out.

Around the Property

The Holler sits on beautiful land with plenty to explore. You'll find a disc golf course on the property, cozy gathering spaces inside, and a fire pit perfect for evening conversations under the stars.

Nearby, the Pisgah National Forest offers hiking, waterfalls, and some of the best whitewater rafting in the region (check out NOC if you're feeling adventurous).

Local Attractions

Favorite spots around Andrews, NC for quick outings and day trips, organized by distance from The Holler.

Close By (0–25 miles)

Fires Creek Recreation Area (≈ 20 min). A winding, scenic drive that opens into a peaceful, creekside playground—ideal for a morning hike, a quiet picnic, or a dip in a cold mountain swimming hole.

Heritage Park Trail & Disc Golf Course (≈ 5 min). An easy, in-town stroll that’s perfect for sunset laps or a low-key game of disc golf before dinner.

Local history spots (≈ 5 min). The Franklin Pierce Cover House and the Valleytown Cultural Arts Center are quick stops that add a little local color to your day.

Easy Day Trip (25–60 miles)

Lake Nantahala (≈ 45 min). Sparkling water, mountain views, and a relaxed pace—perfect for a kayak rental, a picnic by the shore, or a lazy afternoon on the dock.

Nantahala Outdoor Center (NOC) (≈ 55 min). The region’s go-to spot for whitewater rafting, riverside hangs, and post-adventure snacks.

Wayah Bald & Bartram Trail (≈ 55 min). Classic Appalachian views with cool breezes at the top—great for sunrise or sunset hikers.

Great Smoky Mountains Railroad (Bryson City) (≈ 45 min). A scenic train ride that follows the river and delivers effortless mountain magic.

Cherohala Skyway (≈ 50 min to Robbinsville access). One of the most beautiful drives in the Southeast, with overlooks that beg for photo stops.

Full Day Trip (60–120 miles)

Great Smoky Mountains National Park (≈ 1 hr 20 min to Cherokee/Oconaluftee). Waterfalls, wildlife, and iconic trailheads—pack a lunch and make a day of it.

Tail of the Dragon (≈ 1 hr 20 min to Deals Gap). A legendary, curvy mountain drive for car and motorcycle lovers looking for a thrill.

Tip: Drive times are one-way estimates from Andrews. Mountain roads, traffic, and weather can add time—double-check with GPS before you go.

Before You Go

We don't expect you to deep clean, but a little tidiness goes a long way:

  • Wash and put away any dishes you used
  • Bag up trash and leave it by the cans
  • Strip the beds and leave linens in a pile
  • Do a quick walkthrough for any personal items
  • Lock up and make sure all doors are secure
